
Favor Placing a Limit (Codes 01-20)

01: Abuse of the System / Frivolous Lawsuits: Responses mentioning that the system is abused, people are "sue happy," lawsuits are frivolous, or that people lie to get more money.
02: High Awards Harm Businesses/Economy: Reasons stating that large awards can bankrupt or harm businesses.
03: Increases Costs for Everyone (e.g., Insurance): Mentions that large jury awards drive up insurance premiums or the cost of goods and services for all consumers.
04: Difficult/Impossible to Quantify: Responses that state pain and suffering are subjective, emotional, and cannot be truly quantified with a monetary value.
05: Lawyers Benefit Disproportionately: The belief that lawyers are the primary beneficiaries of large awards, not the plaintiffs.
06: Awards are Excessively High / Unreasonable: Statements that the awarded amounts are often "crazy," "out of control," "ridiculous," or disproportionate to the actual damages suffered.
07: Juries are Emotional / Unqualified: The idea that juries are swayed by emotion rather than facts, are not qualified to make these decisions, or that there is too much variability in their judgments.
20: Other Favor: Other reasons in favor of a limit, such as creating structure or ensuring fairness and equality in awards.


Oppose Placing a Limit (Codes 21-40)

21: Cannot Put a Price on Life, Pain, or Suffering: The belief that human life, pain, and emotional distress are priceless and cannot or should not be capped by an arbitrary monetary limit.
22: Need for Corporate/Wrongdoer Accountability: Responses stating that large awards are necessary to hold large corporations accountable for their actions, punish wrongdoing, and deter future harm.
23: Each Case is Unique / Should be Judged on Merit: The argument that every situation is different and should be evaluated by a jury on a case-by-case basis, rather than being restricted by a universal cap.
24: Limits Don't Account for Future Needs or Inflation: Concerns that a fixed cap would not account for the victim's long-term needs, the rising cost of living, or inflation over time.
25: Trust the Jury / Current System: Expression of faith in the jury system to determine a fair and appropriate amount based on the evidence presented.
26: Limits Only Benefit Corporations/Insurance Companies: The belief that placing a cap on damages primarily serves to protect the financial interests of large corporations and insurance companies at the expense of individuals.
27: Victims Deserve to be Compensated: A general sentiment that people who have been harmed deserve to be compensated for their suffering, and a limit would be unfair to them.
40: Other Oppose: Other reasons for opposing a limit, such as the idea that capping damages is morally wrong or that it could reduce access to justice for less advantaged people.


Other Codes

96: Other/Confused/Irrelevant: Responses that are completely irrelevant, demonstrate a clear misunderstanding of the question, or provide a response that argues for both sides without a clear position.
99: Don't Know / Refused: Responses where the person explicitly states they don't know, are not sure, or refuse to answer.
